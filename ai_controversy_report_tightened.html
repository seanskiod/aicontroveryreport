Key Conclusions

All AI engines recognize the campaign as problematic, with none defending it outright. This suggests a broad consensus in training data about the sensitivity of eugenic-related messaging in marketing.

Spectrum from Claude's blunt assessment ("white supremacist beauty ideals") to ChatGPT's diplomatic framing. This reflects different approaches to handling controversial topics.

3. Corporate Accountability Emphasis

All engines specifically addressed diversity gaps in decision-making as a key factor, indicating strong training on how homogeneous teams create blind spots in sensitive campaigns.

Every model emphasized historical connections to eugenics and racialized beauty standards, suggesting this context is well-represented in training data across all platforms.
